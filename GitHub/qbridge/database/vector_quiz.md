## Question 8 ‚Äî Magnitude: 2D

What is the magnitude of $\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}$?

- A) 7
- B) 5
- C) 25
- D) $\sqrt{7}$

**Correct Answer:** B  *(Using Pythagorean theorem: $\sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9+16} = \sqrt{25} = 5$)*

**Tags:** `vectors`, `magnitude`

## Question 9 ‚Äî Magnitude: Higher Dimensions (Short Answer)

Given $\vec{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 7 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \\ 3 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix}$, find $|\vec{a} + \vec{b}|$.

> *Hint: First compute $\vec{a} + \vec{b}$, then take its magnitude.*

**Correct Answer:** $\vec{a} + \vec{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 6 \\ 10 \\ 12 \end{bmatrix}$, so $|\vec{a}+\vec{b}| = \sqrt{3^2+6^2+10^2+12^2} = \sqrt{9+36+100+144} = \sqrt{289} = 17$

**Tags:** `vectors`, `magnitude`, `higher-dimensions`, `addition`
